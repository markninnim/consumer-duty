Checking your understanding: Does your new mortgage have a Fixed or Variable interest rate?
Fixed is where your repayments will not change for an initial period. Variable, sometimes known as a tracker mortgage, where repayments may change in line with the Bank of England base rate.
If you are unsure of the type of mortgage, you will be able to find this information within the suitability report mentioned earlier. Please have a look at this while we get your adviser to contact you to explain further.
Question 5 of 10
Did your Adviser explain they'll review your mortgage before the initial rate expires?
For example, in 5 years time if you went for a 5 year fixed rate mortgage.
Just so you know. Your Adviser will stay in contact with you and when the time comes will search the entire market to find the most suitable deal for your circumstances, ensuring you do not end up on your lender's standard variable rate and paying more money.
Question 6 of 10
Did your Adviser explain that your home may be repossessed if you do not keep up repayments on your mortgage?
Important information. If you do not keep up repayments on your mortgage, you may become homeless. Your Adviser can advise you on how this can be prevented.

It is important that your income has sufficient protection to make sure you are always able to repay your mortgage and other debts. Please speak to your Adviser who will guide you on this.
